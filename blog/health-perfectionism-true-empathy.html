Health, Perfectionism & True Empathy

April 10, 2025

Hello my friend,

Welcome back.

Our health is our foundation.

It's the table that everything else sits on.

It’s the best investment we can make.

Yet we often place our focus elsewhere.

Too busy, too little time.

We’ll do it down the line.

We don’t prioritise the thing that bring will us the greatest rewards.

The thing that makes every single thing in our lives better.

Both now and in the long term.

I have met thousands of people who say they have no time to exercise.

Yet many of them admit to having four or five hours of daily screen time.

Go figure.

Now isn’t the best time they might say.

I just don’t enjoy it others proclaim.

Yes it can feel challenging to get started or to develop some new habits or behaviours.

But the repercussions of neglecting them are far harder.

It’s easy to view health as something we can focus on later.

That we can add into our routine once everything else is well aligned.

When the time, energy or resources are available.

Yet they never arrive.

There is always a mañana. Until there isn’t.

Saying we don’t have time is an excuse.

It’s just not a priority.

I know because I have plenty of experience saying “I don’t have the time” for various things in my life.

I was lying to myself.

They simply weren’t important enough to me to make the time.

Or they felt a little too uncomfortable that I preferred not to.

Mañana.

Imagine there was a free pill you could take which increased longevity and heart health more than anything else? Exercise.

Imagine there was a free pill that decreased stress, increased energy and boosted the immune system more than anything else? Sleep.

These two alone are fundamental to quality health yet sometimes we don’t give them the respect and attention they deserve.

In Daniel Pink’s book ‘The Power of Regret’ he found that one of the biggest regrets of the older generation was they wish they had paid more attention to their health.

Many stuck at home with limited mobility and various conditions, they could see clearly with hindsight the investment they wish they’d made earlier.

All the money in the bank and the time in the world became irrelevant.

Our health is our number one resource and asset.

The more we invest in our health the better everything gets.

It’s not just the best thing we can do for ourselves but for everyone else around us.

We show up on a different level when we feel good.

The job or career doesn’t matter if we don’t have our health.

Our relationships, family and social life can barely function without it.

Without the table there is nowhere to build or to sit.

Nothing else matters without it.

As Confucius said, a healthy man wants a thousand things, a sick man only wants one.

Things I’m learning

Perfectionist problems.

Perfectionism is divided into three categories. Self-oriented perfectionism implies the tendency to expect perfection of oneself. Other-oriented perfectionism implies the tendency to be demanding of others. And socially-prescribed perfectionism implies the belief that others expect you to be perfect. So, preoccupied with approval, the perfectionist often becomes a people-pleaser, obsessed with the purity of some specific role, despite lacking a preference to embody it for its own sake.

If you cannot decide, the answer is no.

“And the reason is, modern society is full of options. There are tons and tons of options. We live on a planet of seven billion people, and we are connected to everybody on the internet. There are hundreds of thousands of careers available to you. There are so many choices.

You’re biologically not built to realize how many choices there are. Historically, we’ve all evolved in tribes of 150 people. When someone comes along, they may be your only option for a partner.

When you choose something, you get locked in for a long time. Starting a business may take ten years. You start a relationship that will be five years or maybe more. You move to a city for ten to twenty years. These are very, very long-lived decisions. It’s very, very important we only say yes when we are pretty certain. You’re never going to be absolutely certain, but you’re going to be very certain.

If you find yourself creating a spreadsheet for a decision with a list of yes’s and nos, pros and cons, checks and balances, why this is good or bad…forget it. If you cannot decide, the answer is no. - Naval

The meaning of empathy.

“The beauty of empathy is that it doesn’t demand that you agree with the other person’s ideas” - Chris Voss

Question

How much time are you spending thinking about change compared to doing something about it?

That’s all for this week.

Thank you for reading.
