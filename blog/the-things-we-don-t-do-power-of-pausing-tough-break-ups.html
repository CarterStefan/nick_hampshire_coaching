The Things We Don't Do, Power of Pausing & Tough Break-ups

April 03, 2025

Hello my friend,

To do or not to do.

This week I’m thinking about the things I’m not doing.

We are all sold the strategies, habits and hacks we should being doing in order to achieve, be happy, or you name it.

If you want to be (fill in the gap) you must being doing X and Y alongside taking a good dose of supplement Z.

Do this and do that.

If you’re not doing you’re not progressing.

It’s exciting. It’s give us a shot of dopamine. It sells.

We associate happiness or fulfillment, peace or success with the things that we do in our lives.

But I’m more interested in the things we don’t do.

Those are the things which bring us closer to everything we’re seeking.

What we choose not to engage in.

Who we choose not to spend time with.

Where we say no.

The habits, behaviours and decisions we opt-out of.

These are what we should be paying most attention to.

“Success is largely the failures you avoid.” - James Clear

Maybe they are harder to acknowledge and appreciate because they aren’t as glamorous or sexy.

Not particularly exciting, inspiring or serotonin rushing.

Boring, perhaps.

No-one will pat us on the back for them.

They won’t be instagram worthy.

But they are what pay the huge dividends.

Not over-consuming.

Not taking harmful substances.

Not spending time with toxic people.

Not being around negativity.

Not getting injured.

Not engaging in arguments.

Not over-working.

Not retaliating.

Not being distracted.

Not checking the phone every 10 minutes.

Not making hasty purchases.

Not saying yes when you really want to say no.

The decisions we don’t make provide massive rewards which are often overlooked.

They may not be as exciting but they are what move the needle.

Over the long term it will be the certain habits, behaviours and decisions that we avoid that will serve us most.

They are what will bring us everything we think we must do in order to receive them.

We have it backwards.

The things we want the most are a question of the things that we don’t do.

Things I’m learning

Pause to enhance your energy, focus, and creativity.

Recent research underscores the benefits of taking intentional breaks. A systematic review and meta-analysis published in PLOS ONE examined the efficacy of micro-breaks - short pauses of no longer than 10 minutes - on well-being and performance. The findings revealed that micro-breaks significantly boost vigor and reduce fatigue. Moreover, longer breaks were associated with greater performance improvements, particularly in tasks requiring creativity and clerical work.

You know better.

“If you’re so smart why aren’t you happy?”- Naval

Being in a toxic relationship with our phone.

“When all you have is a hammer, every problem looks like a nail: the more I used my phone to navigate my life, the less capable I felt of navigating life without my phone.” - Catherine Price

Question

If mistakes are the path for improvement, why aren’t you making more?

Life hack

Drink a large glass of water before your morning coffee. Caffeine spikes cortisol which increases stress and sometimes feelings of anxiety. Wait an hour or at least fully hydrate first.

Caveat: Taking caffeine straight before a workout can provide benefits to training.
